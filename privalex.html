Design Reference Guide


Recommended Reference Websites



1. McKinsey & Company — www.mckinsey.com

Global benchmark for professional services firm websites.

McKinsey.com is the single most influential professional services website in the world. It sets the standard for how an advisory firm presents thought leadership, services, and expertise without looking like a brochure. The site is content-led, insight-driven, and radiates authority without arrogance.

What PrivaLex Should Adopt from McKinsey

  Thought leadership as the homepage anchor — not services lists, but insight. McKinsey leads with ideas, not offerings

  Clean, restrained typography — large serif or bold sans headlines, generous white space, no clutter

  Featured authors and experts with real credentials and faces — humanises a technically complex offering

  Global / local navigation — 'Our Locations' with city-specific content (Lagos and London for PrivaLex)

  Insight hubs — white papers, articles, and perspectives organised by theme, not just date

  No stock photography of people in suits — original, purposeful imagery only

  Mobile-first layout that maintains gravitas on all screen sizes


2. OneTrust — www.onetrust.com

Best-in-class website for a data protection and privacy technology company.

OneTrust is the world's largest privacy, security, and compliance platform. Their website is a masterclass in how a technically complex offering can be communicated with clarity and confidence. They convert complexity into trust signals without over-simplifying.

What PrivaLex Should Adopt from OneTrust

  Trust bar at the top — key accreditations, recognitions, and partner logos visible immediately

  By-industry navigation — 'Financial Services', 'Healthcare', 'Technology' — speaks directly to each buyer

  Clear value proposition on every page — not just 'about us', but 'what you get when you work with us'

  Demo / consultation CTA in the primary navigation — always visible, never buried

  Use of statistics and recognition badges to establish authority.

  Clean product / service cards with short descriptions — no jargon, no padding

  Resource library separated from the homepage — keeps the main site clean


3. WilmerHale — www.wilmerhale.com

Premier law firm — perfect model for a privacy and regulatory advisory firm.

WilmerHale is one of the top cybersecurity and privacy law firms in the world. Their website is intelligent, substantive, and commands premium positioning. It communicates depth without verbosity.

What PrivaLex Should Adopt from WilmerHale

  Practice area pages with genuine substance — not bulleted lists, but real paragraphs that show expertise

  Awards and recognition section that is prominent without being boastful

  Insight articles tied directly to practice areas — every article reinforces a service

  Clean, dark-navy premium colour palette that signals seriousness

  Client-facing language — 'We help clients navigate...' not 'We offer services in...'


4. Freshfields — www.freshfields.com

Elite global law firm with exceptional modern website UX — best navigation model.

Freshfields recently relaunched with one of the best professional services websites in existence. The navigation is exceptional — complex service offerings made simple, sector expertise front and centre, and a thought leadership section that actually gets read.

What PrivaLex Should Adopt from Freshfields

  Mega-menu navigation with sector and service groupings — users find what they need in one click

  Hero section that rotates between insight topics, not static brand statements

  Bold, confident homepage statement — short, declarative, memorable

  Footer that reinforces global credentials and key links without overcrowding

  Dark mode option or dark hero sections that create visual drama and premium feel

  No clip-art or generic photography — every image is purposeful and high-quality


5. AlixPartners — www.alixpartners.com

Most confidently positioned professional services firm website.

AlixPartners is shorter, sharper, and more confident than most professional services sites. They use few words and make each one count. For PrivaLex's positioning as Africa's leading data protection advisory firm, this directness is exactly the right posture.

What PrivaLex Should Adopt from AlixPartners

  Extreme headline confidence — 'When it really matters' — short, powerful, memorable positioning

  Minimal homepage — no clutter, no excess, pure signal

  Sectors lead the navigation — clients think in sectors, not service lines

  Bold statistics used sparingly but powerfully

  Fast-loading, no animations that slow the experience


6. SANS Institute — www.sans.org

Gold standard for cybersecurity and information security training websites — ideal model for PrivaLex Academy.

SANS is the world's most trusted cybersecurity training institution. Academy website should borrow heavily from SANS in terms of how courses are listed, how pathways are shown, how instructors are profiled, and how credibility is built through every design choice.

What PrivaLex Academy Should Adopt from SANS

  Course finder with filters by topic, level, and delivery format

  Clear pathway visualisation — beginner to expert mapped visually

  Instructor profiles prominent — training credibility is personal, not institutional alone

  Countdown timers for next cohort dates — creates urgency

  Course page structure: Overview > What You Will Learn > Who Should Attend > Schedule > Register

  Trust signals: certification body logos, pass rates, alumni outcomes

  Community feel — forums, alumni network, masterclass archives


7. ISACA — www.isaca.org

Best certification body website — clean course listings and member community.

What PrivaLex Academy Should Adopt from ISACA

  Certification track pages that show the full career journey — not just one credential

  Exam preparation resources linked directly from course pages

  Corporate training packages clearly explained with volume pricing

  Clear accreditation logos and body partnerships shown prominently


Design Language Summary for PrivaLex Websites

Visual Identity Direction — For the Designer

  TYPOGRAPHY: Bold, confident sans-serif for headlines (e.g. Inter, Neue Haas Grotesk, or similar). Clean, readable serif or sans for body. Large type. Never small

  PHOTOGRAPHY: Real people, real settings, real Africa. Lagos skyline, Holborn streets, conference rooms, not stock library images. Dark, editorial photography where used

  ANIMATION: Minimal. Subtle fade-ins on scroll. No spinning logos, no excessive transitions. Speed matters

  MOBILE: Both sites must be perfect on mobile. Nigerian audience is predominantly mobile-first

  TRUST SIGNALS: PECB logo, IIM logo, NBA-ICLE logo, NDPC DPCO certificate — visible on every page, not buried in footers

  CTA BUTTONS: Two primary CTAs always visible — 'Speak to Our Team' (Advisory) and 'Register Now' (Academy). No passive language

  LOADING SPEED: Sub-2-second load time required. No heavy video autoplay on mobile

INTERIM / TRANSITION

Interim and Transition DPO

Short-term DPO cover during recruitment, restructuring, regulatory engagement, or when your incumbent DPO is unavailable. We step in immediately, maintain regulatory compliance posture, and ensure no obligations are missed during the transition. Scoped for weeks or months depending on need.


ENGAGEMENT MODEL 3

PROJECT DPO

Project DPO Support

Scoped delivery for specific compliance initiatives: DPIAs for new products or high-risk processing, vendor governance programmes, privacy policy and notice refresh, NDPC audit preparation, and regulatory remediation projects. Breach-on-Call DPO

School of Data Protection & Privacy — ADVANCED MASTERY — PRIVALEX FLAGSHIP

Advanced Data Protection Practitioner (ADPP)

Duration: 2 Months | 3 Cohorts Per Year   Credential: PrivaLex Academy ADPP Certificate   Ideal For: Practising DPOs, Privacy Consultants, Legal Professionals, Compliance Leads — Nigeria & International

About This Programme

The Advanced Data Protection Practitioner (ADPP) is the only programme of its kind. While certification courses teach the rules, the ADPP builds the judgement needed to apply them. Over two months, participants study all major privacy frameworks side by side — the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023, the EU General Data Protection Regulation, the UK GDPR, the UK Data Protection Act, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA), sector specific guidelines and regulations and ISO/IEC 27701, and develop the practical skills needed to manage data protection programmes, advise organisations, and operate as genuine privacy professionals at international standard. Topics include: conducting DPIAs across jurisdictions, breach management and regulatory notification, drafting privacy policies and consent frameworks, cross-border transfer mechanism design, building DPO programmes inside organisations, privacy-by-design for products and services, and privacy consulting frameworks. This programme is capped at a small cohort size per intake to ensure quality of instruction and individual attention. Graduates of the ADPP are equipped to practice as DPOs, privacy consultants, and advisory professionals at the highest level.

How to Register

ADPP is delivered three times per year. School of Data Protection & Privacy — REGULATORY — NIGERIA

NDPC / IIM Certified Data Protection Officer (CDPO)

Duration: 5 Days + Examination   Credential: IIM CDPO (NDPC-Licensed Credential)   Ideal For: DPOs, Compliance Officers, Legal Teams, Banks, Universities, Government, Hospitals, All Regulated Sectors

About This Programme

The IIM-NDPC Certified Data Protection Officer (CDPO) is Nigeria's primary regulatory data protection certification, accredited by the Institute for Information Management Africa (IIM) and recognised within the NDPC's ecosystem. It is the essential credential for any professional appointed as a Data Protection Officer under the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023. The programme covers the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 in depth, including data subject rights, lawful bases for processing, security obligations, DPIA requirements, breach notification, and NDPC registration and audit obligations. Participants also receive grounding in global privacy principles to contextualise Nigerian law within international best practice. On completion, candidates sit an examination. Our Accreditations

Every credential awarded through PrivaLex Academy is backed by formal accreditation from recognised certification and professional bodies. These are not internal certificates. They are internationally and nationally recognised qualifications that follow graduates throughout their careers.


PECB — Professional Evaluation and Certification Board

PrivaLex Academy is an authorised PECB training partner. All PECB-certified programmes are delivered by authorised trainers, lead to official PECB certifications, and are recognised in 150+ countries. PECB certifications are maintained through CPD and renewed every three years, ensuring the credential retains market value throughout a professional career.

IIM — Institute for Information Management Africa

PrivaLex Academy is an IIM Accredited Training Organisation. The IIM CDPO credential is accredited by the Institute for Information Management Africa and operates within the NDPC's regulatory ecosystem, making it Nigeria's primary regulatory data protection qualification for DPO appointments.

NDPC — Nigeria Data Protection Commission

PrivaLex Academy operates within the NDPC Ecosystem as an approved training partner. Our NDPA-focused programmes are aligned to NDPC guidance and examination standards.

NBA-ICLE — Nigerian Bar Association Institute for Continuing Legal Education

Our monthly Masterclass Series is accredited by the NBA-ICLE, enabling qualifying legal practitioners to earn Mandatory Continuing Legal Education (MCLE) credits through regular attendance. Each session is individually accredited and a certificate is issued after each qualifying session.
